History

Roller Coasters and Theme Parks have been around for a long time! They've gotten more extreme and more intense in the recent years, and designers are continuing to push the limits on these incredible creations!

But that begs the question: How did these contraptions come to be? What was their inspiration? How did they become the intense thrill machines we know today? Let's take a dive and find out!

Unique Beginnings

The origin of Roller Coasters might suprise you. The first "roller coaster" was found in St. Petersburg, Russia as a simple ice ramp that people would ride sleds down! This would be the inspiration for the next leap forward!

A French travel saw this unique concept and built on it, erecting a track with grooves down the middle, allowing a bench with wheels to fit in the groove and run down the track. This was known as"The Promenades-AÃ©riennes" or "The Aerial Walk" in Paris. Also, it ran sidways!

Switchback Railway

Even though roller coasters were inspired by these ice sleds, in the 19th century, technology evolved and people started using Railroad Tracks with cars that were locked to the track. In 1884, a man named La Marcus Thompson, also known as the "Father of the roller Coaster" built the Switchback Railway at Coney Island. This was considered the first modern roller coaster in America. It was extremely simple: You start at the top and ride down a few hills, reaching daunting speeds of 6 miles per hour.

Advancements throughout the Decades

The Switchback railway was built over 2 centuries ago! In 2024, roller coaster technology has improved immensely! There are now many types of roller coasters, made from different materials and that provide unique experiences! Here are a few:

Wooden Coasters
Classic roller coasters made from wood
Steel Coasters
A more modern take on the concept, providing a smoother, more intense experience. Oh yeah, you can go upside down now!
Inverted Coasters
Instead of sitting above the track, you now hang below the track!
Stand-Up Coasters
Yes, there are indeed coasters that are designed for you to stand up!
